ABOUT

The term “blue chip” didn’t come from Wall Street analysts or economists — it came from the poker table. Blue chips were the most valuable, and the name stuck.

In the 1920s, a Dow Jones employee named Oliver Gingold borrowed the phrase to describe high-priced stocks. Over time, “blue chip” came to mean more than just price — it stood for companies that were large, stable, and trusted. Think Nvidia, Coca-Cola, Apple, or Google.

Today, the idea of blue chips goes far beyond stocks. It’s used for real estate, bonds, collectibles and cryptocurrency.
